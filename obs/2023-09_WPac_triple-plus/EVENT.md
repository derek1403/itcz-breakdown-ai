# Observed monsoon-trough breakdown — West Pacific, 30 Aug – 6 September 2023

**Secondary case** (the cleaner four-in-a-row is `../2024-11_WPac_quad-typhoon/`).

Late August–early September 2023 the WPac monsoon trough was very active and broke into a chain
of vortices: **Saola, Haikui and Kirogi coexisted as typhoons/storms** along the trough from
~30 Aug, with a fourth system (**Yun-yeung**, PAGASA *Ineng*) developing to the east from ~4 Sep.
Compared with Nov 2024 the systems are more N–S spread, so the "discrete chain along one
latitude band" signature is less clean — but the simultaneous multi-vortex breakdown is clear.

## The vortices
| Storm | ~position (early Sep) | Notes |
|-------|-----------------------|-------|
| **Saola** | near Luzon → SCS | intense; landfall Guangdong 2 Sep |
| **Haikui** | Philippine Sea | typhoon 1 Sep; hit Taiwan 3 Sep, Fujian 4 Sep |
| **Kirogi** | east of the others | weakened to TD ~2 Sep, remnants near Japan |
| **Yun-yeung** (Ineng) | NE of PAR | formed ~4 Sep, intensified toward Japan |

## Roll-up (00Z), tropical WPac ~108–163°E, 0–25°N
- 30–31 Aug — Saola (compact, near Luzon/SCS) + a broad convective trough east of the Philippines.
- 1–2 Sep — **Saola + Haikui + Kirogi simultaneously** along the trough (3 vortices).
- 3–5 Sep — Saola → Guangdong, Haikui → Taiwan, Kirogi NE, **Yun-yeung** spinning up to the east.
